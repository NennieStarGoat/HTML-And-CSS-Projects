Nalbinding

Nalbinding is a fabric creation technique predating both knitting and crochet, with the earliest extant examples of it being found in Nahal Hemar Cave, in the Judean Desert, and is dated from 6500 BCE.

There are many different types of stitches to be made, but all use short lengths (2 m) of yarn, and a thick needle, which was traditionally made of wood, bone or antler.
